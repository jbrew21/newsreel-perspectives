The voices we track

We monitor commentators, creators, and journalists across the political spectrum — people with large audiences who regularly shape how millions understand the news. Inclusion isn't endorsement. The goal is to show you how the same story looks through different lenses, so you can think for yourself.

Criteria: 500K+ followers on at least one platform, regularly comments on current events, represents a distinct perspective not already covered. We pull from X, YouTube, Bluesky, TikTok, and Instagram daily.

How it works

Daily collection. Every day, we pull recent posts from each voice across 5 platforms: X, YouTube, Bluesky, TikTok, and Instagram. No content is altered or editorialized — quotes flow directly from the source.

Topic categorization. AI reads each post and tags it with a news topic (e.g. "iran-war", "ai-policy", "immigration"). This lets us match voices to stories when you search.

Argument clusters, not left/right. When you search a story, we don't slap a "left" or "right" label on each voice. Instead, AI analyzes what each person is actually saying and groups them into 4-6 argument clusters — like "anti-war right", "pro-intervention hawk", or "accountability focused." This reveals the real fractures: two people on the same political "side" often take very different positions on the same story.

Why this matters. Most media tools sort voices into a left/right binary. But that flattens reality. Tucker Carlson and Ben Shapiro are both conservative — but on Iran, one is anti-war and the other is pro-intervention. That difference is the insight. Our clusters show you what people actually think, not which team they're on.

Inclusion isn't endorsement. We track voices across the full spectrum, including some you may strongly disagree with. The goal is not to amplify any particular viewpoint — it's to make sure you're not only hearing one side.
